About the Series

Parks and Recreation is a political comedy television sitcom series that follows the misadventures Leslie Knope (Amy Poehler), an optimistic and energetic bureaucrat working in the Parks Department in Pawnee, Indiana. Supporting cast members include Aziz Ansari, Nick Offerman, Rashida Jones, Chris Pratt, Aubrey Plaza, Rob Lowe, Adam Scott, Jim O’Heir, Retta, and Billy Eichner. The series aired on NBC from April 9, 2009 to February 24, 2015 for 125 episodes over seven seasons. After receiving mixed reviews in its first season, Parks and Recreation underwent changes in writing and tone, going on to generate a devoted fan base and high critical praise.

Unlike other popular comedies that rely on misanthropic or disgruntled characters, Parks and Rec maintains a bright tone by developing heartfelt relationships between characters who, despite their personal flaws and quirks, take care of one another and mean well in the work they do. Over the course of the series, the characters grow and develop in relatable, believable ways, while still retaining the distinctive qualities that make them who they are.

What’s more, the show is just plain funny; its ensemble cast is made up of trained improv comedians who develop an offbeat and energized chemistry with one another. The series maintains long running inside jokes, and consistent viewing allows for audience members to feel out the various rhythms and patterns of each character’s idiosyncrasies. The result is a sense that one has developed an intimate connection with this odd bunch of do-gooders, and watching an episode or two begins to feel like spending time with good friends.
